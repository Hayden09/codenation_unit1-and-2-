1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Lack of cohesion, and this can apply to a number of areas from the actual plan all the way to presentation. In terms of group projects communication is paramount in order to make sure one vision is applied to a project. Getting bogged down by 40 different ideas being implemented outside of the first one or two planning sessions will on serve to distract at best with the focus on developing them, to frustrating and damaging the development process such as breaking elements of a project and causing general stress and demotivation, reducing the developers effectiveness at performing. Saving project files incorrectly will also cause issues, which should be noted if you’re the kind of person who does submissions last minute.

External problems that can cause a project to become impacted can be funding. If a third party has a client pull the rug out from under them, then that automatically kills months of work and could potentially really harm a business if they were riding on the projects funding (With said funding being provided in chunks). There’s also the unfortunate element of server and system failures when work hasn’t been saved, or the building where a project is hosted is rendered temporarily inaccessible for any reason. It’s important to have a backlog or separate catalogue of what work has been created, but it’s paramount to ensure it’s secure, as one of the biggest external dangers to a projects development (especially if said project has been collaboratively done entirely through an online resource) it’s malicious third parties sabotaging the work, stealing or removing files that are vital to completion.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

Ignorance of copyright law is a surefire way to have your project obliterated before your eyes. When producing your project the basics are in order: make it legally distinct. Regardless of if it’s your own idea or whether you’re really wanting to copy another idea, if it’s not legally it’s own you’ll be faced with cease and desist notices and potential lawsuits.

Another law to consider is GDPR (known in some cases as data protection and failure to comply with it’s guidelines is a sure-fire way of being fined. To quote the EGNYTE page: “Under GDPR, EU citizens may submit a Subject Access Request (SAR) to find out which Personally Identifiable Information (PII) a company has about them.”, this means if your own project involves storing people’s information then you have to comply with a customer or persons request to give them all the information that you have on them, and more importantly to comply with any requests to remove their information from your systems. There should always be a legally binding “opt out” option for a customer to select if you happen to require their personal information for anything. Even if it’s as simple as hosting their pictures on an app you’ve made, you should never have legal permission to keep those things forever unless the work has been made for you and you’ve paid for it, and signed a contract stating that said images fall under your assets and you can do with them how you please (Although this is getting into a whole other type of sphere).

Data protection as a term also covers the obvious, if you have someones information, take every measure possible to ensure that information is safe, secure, and only truly accessible within the company itself. Data leaks can be catastrophic, and publicly air anything from phone numbers, emails, passwords, to the biggest of issues like bank account information.

There’s also the computer fraud and abuse act to consider when working on a project, CFAA is generally targeted towards hackers who are involved with data theft, but failure to declare what information you’re accessing when working with people is guaranteed to have them come after you. So in a sense this law ties in with GDPR. Declare what information you’re going to take, and always have an opt out option.
